**Title: Cross-Sectional Dispersion of Realized Volatility as a Leading Indicator for VIX Regime Transitions**

This study quantifies the structural divergence between realized sectoral volatility and implied market volatility by calculating the 21-day rolling cross-sectional standard deviation (CSSD) of log-returns across 10 stable sector ETFs. Rather than modeling directional returns, we define the "Sectoral Volatility Dispersion" (SVD) as the primary feature to identify shifts in market-wide risk regimes. We hypothesize that an expansion in SVD—representing idiosyncratic volatility clustering—precedes significant regime shifts in `^VIX`, even when market beta remains stable. The model utilizes a threshold-based regime detection framework, regressing `^VIX` innovations against lagged SVD values to distinguish between systemic market shocks and sector-specific decoupling. By excluding `XLC` and `XLRE` to maintain data uniformity, we eliminate bias from missing observations, ensuring the SVD metric remains a stable measure of internal market tension. This approach provides a robust, non-parametric signal for volatility regime changes that avoids the multicollinearity inherent in PCA-based factor models.
